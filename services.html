HDPE Sheet

HDPE (high-density polyethylene) sheet membrane is a type of sheet membrane used for waterproofing applications. It is a highly durable and versatile material that is used in various construction projects, including roofs, plaza decks, foundation walls, and tunnels. HDPE sheet membranes are composed of high-density polyethylene, which is a thermoplastic polymer that has a high strength-to-density ratio, excellent chemical resistance, and low moisture absorption. The membrane is typically supplied in rolls, which are easy to handle and install.

HDPE sheet membranes are installed by first preparing the surface to be waterproofed by cleaning and smoothing it out. The membrane is then unrolled and cut to the appropriate size, and the edges are sealed using specialized adhesives or heat welding techniques. The membrane is then secured in place using mechanical fasteners.

HDPE sheet membrane is a reliable and effective waterproofing solution that offers excellent durability and chemical resistance. It is a popular choice for a variety of construction projects due to its ease of installation and cost-effectiveness.

Epoxy Injection Grouting

Epoxy injection grouting is a type of waterproofing method that involves injecting epoxy resins into cracks, voids, and gaps in concrete or masonry structures. The process involves injecting the epoxy resin into the cracks under pressure, which helps to fill the voids and form a strong, durable bond.

Epoxy injection grouting is commonly used to repair and waterproof a variety of structures, including buildings, bridges, tunnels, and dams. It can be used to seal cracks in concrete floors, walls, and foundations to prevent water intrusion and to provide additional structural strength.

Terrace Waterproofing

Terrace waterproofing is the process of protecting a terrace or flat roof from water infiltration by applying a waterproof layer on the surface. The waterproofing layer is typically made of materials such as bitumen, polyurethane, or acrylic coatings. Water infiltration can cause damage to the terrace, the underlying structure, and even the interior of the building. Therefore, proper terrace waterproofing is essential to prevent these issues and ensure the longevity of the building.

RCC

RCC stands for Reinforced Cement Concrete, which is a composite material made up of cement, aggregates (such as sand and gravel), water, and reinforcement (usually steel bars or mesh). RCC is widely used in construction as it is strong, durable, and versatile.

The reinforcement in RCC is added to enhance the strength and durability of the concrete. The steel bars or mesh are placed in the formwork before the concrete is poured, and they help to resist tensile forces that may develop in the concrete. The concrete, in turn, provides compressive strength to the structure.

Epoxy Top Coat

Epoxy top coats create a dense protective finish that improves durability, surface life, and resistance to wear. This treatment is especially useful in areas that face moisture, movement, and repeated use.

The final coat also improves the visual finish of the surface by giving it a more uniform, sealed, and easy-to-maintain appearance.
